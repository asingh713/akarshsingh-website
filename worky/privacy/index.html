1. Who We Are

Worky is developed and operated by Akarsh Singh, SD Solutions NC, based in North Carolina, USA (the "Controller," "we," "us," or "our"). We are the data controller responsible for your personal information under applicable privacy law, including the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), and Quebec Law 25.

Our designated Privacy Officer is Akarsh Singh. For all privacy-related inquiries, data subject requests, or concerns, contact us via akarshsingh.com/contact.

2. Data We Collect

We collect only what is necessary for the app to function. No advertising identifiers, no behavioral tracking, no analytics SDKs.

Account identifiers

If you sign in with Apple, we receive a unique anonymized user identifier and, only if you choose to share it, your name and email address. If you sign in with Google, we receive your Google display name and email address via Google's official Sign-In SDK. These are stored in your device's Keychain and synced privately to your iCloud account. If you continue as a Guest, only a "Guest" label is stored locally.

Personal information you enter

Name, email address, age, height, and body weight — entered voluntarily in Settings. BMI is computed locally from height and weight and never sent anywhere.

Health and fitness data

Workout days, exercise names, sets, reps, weights, completion dates, body weight log entries, and daily notes — all created by you. With your explicit permission, the app reads resting heart rate and date of birth from Apple Health, and writes body weight entries and workout summaries back to Apple Health.

Exercise search queries

When you use the exercise search feature, the text you type (e.g. "bench press") is sent to wger.de, a free open-source exercise database. No account details, device identifiers, or personal information are included.

What we do NOT collect

Advertising identifiers (IDFA), precise location, contacts, photos, camera, microphone, crash reports, usage analytics, or any device fingerprinting data.

4. Health Data — Special Category Processing

Under GDPR Article 9, health data is a "special category" requiring heightened protection and explicit consent. Worky processes health-related data including body weight history, workout logs, BMI, and (with your permission) resting heart rate and date of birth from Apple Health.

Your explicit consent is the basis for this processing. You provide consent by: (a) granting HealthKit permissions when prompted by iOS, and (b) voluntarily entering personal health information in the app. You may withdraw consent at any time by revoking HealthKit access in iOS Settings or by deleting all data via Settings → Sign Out.

This data is stored solely on your device and in your private iCloud account. We have no access to it. It is never used for advertising, profiling, or any purpose other than powering Worky's features for you.

5. How Your Data Is Stored

On your device: All workout data, personal information, and notes are stored locally using Apple's SwiftData framework in an encrypted database. Account credentials are stored in your device's Keychain, encrypted and protected by your passcode and biometrics.

iCloud sync (optional): If you sign in with Apple or Google, your data syncs to your private iCloud account via Apple's CloudKit. Only you can access it. SD Solutions NC has no access to your iCloud data, no keys to decrypt it, and no servers storing copies of it.

Apple Health: With your permission, Worky reads and writes data to Apple Health on your device. This is governed by Apple's privacy policy and your iOS Health settings.

No SD Solutions NC servers: We operate no backend servers, databases, or cloud infrastructure that stores your personal data.

8. How Long We Keep Your Data

  • Active use: Data is stored on your device and in your private iCloud account for as long as you use the app.
  • Sign out: All data is permanently deleted from your device and iCloud when you sign out via Settings → Sign Out. iCloud deletion typically propagates within minutes.
  • App deletion: Deleting the app removes all local data. iCloud data remains until you sign out first, or delete it via iOS Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Storage.
  • Keychain: Account credentials are deleted on sign out. Some iOS versions may retain Keychain items after app deletion; remove these via Settings → Password & Security if needed.
