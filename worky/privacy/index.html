Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026  ·  Effective for all versions of Worky

1. Who We Are

Worky is developed and operated by Akarsh Singh, SD Solutions NC, based in North Carolina, USA (the "Controller," "we," "us," or "our"). We are the data controller responsible for your personal information under applicable privacy law, including the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), and Quebec Law 25.

Our designated Privacy Officer is Akarsh Singh. For all privacy-related inquiries, data subject requests, or concerns, contact us via akarshsingh.com/contact.

2. Data We Collect

We collect only what is necessary for the app to function. No advertising identifiers, no behavioral tracking, no analytics SDKs.

Account identifiers

If you sign in with Apple, we receive a unique anonymized user identifier and, only if you choose to share it, your name and email address. If you sign in with Google, we receive your Google display name and email address via Google's official Sign-In SDK. These are stored in your device's Keychain and synced privately to your iCloud account. If you continue without an account (Guest mode), only a "Guest" label is stored locally.

Personal information you enter

Name, email address, age, height, and body weight — entered voluntarily in Settings to personalize the experience. BMI is computed locally from height and weight and never sent anywhere.

Health and fitness data

Workout days, exercise names, sets, reps, weights, completion dates, body weight log entries, and daily notes — all created by you within the app. With your explicit permission, the app reads resting heart rate and date of birth from Apple Health, and writes body weight entries and workout summaries back to Apple Health.

Exercise search queries

When you use the exercise search feature, the text you type (e.g. "bench press") is sent to wger.de, a free open-source exercise database. No account details, device identifiers, or personal information are included in these requests.

What we do NOT collect

Advertising identifiers (IDFA), precise location, contacts, photos, camera, microphone, crash reports, usage analytics, or any device fingerprinting data.

4. Health Data — Special Category Processing

Under GDPR Article 9, health data is a "special category" of personal data requiring heightened protection and explicit consent. Worky processes health-related data including body weight history, workout logs, BMI, and (with your permission) resting heart rate and date of birth from Apple Health.

Your explicit consent is the basis for this processing. You provide consent by: (a) granting HealthKit permissions when prompted by iOS, and (b) voluntarily entering personal health information in the app. You may withdraw consent at any time by revoking HealthKit access in iOS Settings or by deleting all data via Settings → Sign Out within the app.

This data is stored solely on your device and in your private iCloud account. We have no access to it. It is never used for advertising, profiling, or any purpose other than powering Worky's features for you.

5. How Your Data Is Stored

On your device: All workout data, personal information, and notes are stored locally using Apple's SwiftData framework in an encrypted database on your device. Account credentials (name, email, user identifier) are stored in your device's Keychain, which is encrypted and protected by your device passcode and biometrics.

iCloud sync (optional): If you sign in with Apple or Google, your data syncs to your private iCloud account via Apple's CloudKit. This sync is private — only you can access it. SD Solutions NC has no access to your iCloud data, no keys to decrypt it, and no servers that store copies of it.

Apple Health: With your permission, Worky reads and writes data to Apple Health on your device. This data is governed by Apple's privacy policy and your iOS Health settings.

No SD Solutions NC servers: We operate no backend servers, databases, or cloud infrastructure that stores your personal data.

6. Third-Party Services

Worky interacts with the following third-party services. We do not sell your data to any of them, and none receive your personal information for advertising purposes.

Apple (CloudKit, Sign in with Apple, HealthKit)

Apple processes iCloud sync and authentication. Your data in CloudKit is stored in Apple's private database and is not accessible to us. Governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

Google (Sign in with Google)

If you choose Google Sign-In, your name and email are obtained from Google via OAuth. Google processes this authentication on its servers. Governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

wger.de (Exercise Database)

Exercise name searches are sent to wger.de, a free open-source exercise database hosted in Germany. Only the search term you type is transmitted — no personal information, account data, or device identifiers are included. Governed by wger's Privacy Policy.

Google Fonts (this website only)

This privacy policy page loads the Plus Jakarta Sans typeface from Google Fonts, which may log your IP address as part of the request. This applies to the website only, not the Worky iOS app. Governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

7. International Data Transfers

SD Solutions NC is based in the United States (North Carolina). Some of our third-party service providers process data in the United States and other countries. For users in the EU/EEA, these transfers are protected as follows:

  • Apple: relies on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and the EU-US Data Privacy Framework for transfers from the EU.
  • Google: relies on SCCs and the EU-US Data Privacy Framework for transfers from the EU.
  • wger.de: hosted in Germany — data does not leave the European Economic Area.

For Canadian users, transfers to the United States are made pursuant to PIPEDA's cross-border transfer provisions, with reliance on contractual protections with our service providers.

8. How Long We Keep Your Data

Because we do not operate servers or databases, your data is retained entirely under your control:

  • Active use: Data is stored on your device and in your private iCloud account for as long as you use the app.
  • Sign out: All data is permanently deleted from your device and iCloud account when you sign out via Settings → Sign Out. iCloud deletion typically propagates within minutes.
  • App deletion: Deleting the app removes all local data. iCloud data associated with the app remains in your iCloud account until you sign out first or delete it via iOS Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Storage.
  • Keychain: Account credentials in the Keychain are deleted on sign out. Some iOS versions may retain Keychain items after app deletion; you can remove these via Settings → Password & Security if needed.

9. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you have the following rights regarding your personal data. To exercise any right, contact us via akarshsingh.com/contact. We will respond within 30 days (EU: within 1 month as required by GDPR).

EU/EEA Residents (GDPR)

  • Right of access (Art. 15): Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16): Request correction of inaccurate data. Most data can be corrected directly in the app's Settings.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17): Request deletion of your data. You can exercise this immediately in-app via Settings → Sign Out, which permanently deletes all data.
  • Right to restriction (Art. 18): Request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20): Request your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object (Art. 21): Object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)): Withdraw consent for health data processing at any time by revoking HealthKit permissions in iOS Settings or signing out of the app.
  • Right to lodge a complaint: You have the right to lodge a complaint with your local EU data protection supervisory authority.

California Residents (CCPA/CPRA)

  • Right to know: Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information collected about you.
  • Right to delete: Request deletion of personal information (exercisable in-app via sign out).
  • Right to correct: Request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing: We do not sell or share your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. No opt-out is required, but we honor all requests.
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information: We use sensitive information (health data) only to provide app functionality.
  • Right to non-discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any CCPA rights.

Canadian Residents (PIPEDA / Quebec Law 25)

  • Right to access: Request access to personal information we hold about you and how it is used.
  • Right to correct: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Withdraw consent for collection and use of your personal information, subject to legal or contractual restrictions.
  • Right to complain: Canadian residents may file a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC) at priv.gc.ca. Quebec residents may also file with the Commission d'accès à l'information (CAI) at cai.gouv.qc.ca.

Pursuant to Quebec Law 25, our Privacy Officer is Akarsh Singh. Contact details are provided in Section 13.

11. Data Breach Notification

Because we do not store your personal data on any SD Solutions NC servers, the most likely source of a data security incident would be at a third-party service provider (Apple, Google) rather than within our direct control. However, in the event of a security incident that affects your personal data:

  • EU/EEA users (GDPR): We will notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach where feasible, and will notify affected users without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms.
  • US users (FTC Health Breach Notification Rule): We will notify affected users within 60 days of discovering a breach involving personal health records, and will notify the Federal Trade Commission as required by law.
  • Canadian users (PIPEDA): We will notify the Office of the Privacy Commissioner and affected individuals where a breach creates a real risk of significant harm.
