Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 19, 2026

SD Solutions NC ("we", "us", "our") operates TPRM ("Service"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect information when you use our Service.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account information.  When you create an account, we collect your name, email address, username, and password (stored as a salted hash — we never store your plaintext password).

1.2 Organization data.  We collect information about your organization including its name and the subscription plan associated with it.

1.3 Vendor and assessment data.  We store the vendor information, questionnaire responses, risk scores, attachments, and notes you enter into the Service ("Customer Data"). This data belongs to you — see Section 4.

1.4 Usage data.  We automatically collect information about how you use the Service, including pages visited, features used, and actions taken. This is used to improve the Service and enforce subscription limits.

1.5 Technical data.  We collect IP addresses, browser type, device information, and cookies (session authentication only — see Section 5) when you access the Service.

1.6 Communications.  If you contact us for support, we retain records of that communication.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • –Provide, operate, and maintain the Service
  • –Authenticate users and enforce access controls
  • –Enforce subscription plan limits (vendor counts, AI search quotas)
  • –Process payments via Stripe (we do not store card numbers)
  • –Send in-app notifications relevant to your account
  • –Generate AI-powered vendor research results via Anthropic's API (vendor name and website only)
  • –Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and security incidents
  • –Comply with legal obligations
  • –Improve and develop the Service using aggregated, anonymized usage data

We do not use your Customer Data to train AI models, and we do not sell your data to third parties.

3. Information We Share

We share your information only in the following circumstances:

3.1 Service providers.  We use the following third-party services to operate the platform:

Provider Purpose Data shared
Stripe Payment processing Email, billing info
Anthropic AI vendor research Vendor name, website URL
DigitalOcean Cloud infrastructure and file storage All Customer Data (encrypted at rest)

3.2 Legal requirements.  We may disclose your information if required by law, court order, or governmental authority, or if we believe disclosure is necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of SD Solutions NC, our users, or the public.

3.3 Business transfers.  In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred. We will notify you before your information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

3.4 With your consent.  We may share your information for any other purpose with your explicit consent.

4. Your Data and Your Rights

4.1 Ownership.  You own your Customer Data. We process it only to provide the Service.

4.2 Access and export.  You may export your data at any time from within the Service or by contacting us at privacy@tprm.io.

4.3 Correction.  You may update your account information at any time from account settings.

4.4 Deletion.  You may request deletion of your account and all associated data by contacting privacy@tprm.io. Deletion is completed within 30 days. Some data may be retained as required by law (e.g. billing records).

4.5 Portability.  Upon request, we will provide your Customer Data in a machine-readable format (JSON or CSV).

4.6 GDPR rights (EEA users).  If you are located in the European Economic Area, you have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict processing of, and object to processing of your personal data. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

4.7 CCPA rights (California residents).  California residents have the right to know what personal information we collect, to request deletion, and to opt out of the sale of personal information. We do not sell personal information.

5. Cookies

We use a single session authentication cookie (token) that is:

  • –HTTP-only — not accessible to JavaScript
  • –Secure — transmitted only over HTTPS
  • –SameSite: Lax — protected against cross-site request forgery

We do not use advertising, tracking, or analytics cookies. No third-party cookies are set by the Service.

6. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures including:

  • –TLS encryption for all data in transit
  • –AES-256 encryption for sensitive configuration values at rest
  • –Bcrypt password hashing
  • –HTTP security headers (HSTS, CSP, X-Frame-Options)
  • –Login rate limiting and brute-force protection
  • –Regular security reviews and penetration testing

While we take security seriously, no system is completely secure. We cannot guarantee the absolute security of your data.

7. Data Retention

  • –Active accounts: Customer Data retained for the duration of the subscription
  • –Cancelled accounts: Data retained for 30 days after cancellation, then permanently deleted
  • –Audit logs: Retained for 2 years for security and compliance purposes
  • –Billing records: Retained for 7 years as required by law

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to children under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, contact us at privacy@tprm.io and we will delete it promptly.

9. International Transfers

Our servers are located in the United States. If you access the Service from outside the US, your information will be transferred to and processed in the US. By using the Service, you consent to this transfer. For EEA users, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with GDPR requirements.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of material changes by posting a notice in the Service at least 14 days before changes take effect. Continued use after the effective date constitutes acceptance.
